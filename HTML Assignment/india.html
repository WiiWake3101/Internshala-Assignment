India

  • India, officially the Republic of India, is a country in South Asia.
  • It is the seventh-largest country by land area, the second-most populous country, and the most populous democracy in the world.
  • India has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its diverse landscapes, languages, and traditions.
  • The capital city of India is New Delhi, and the largest city is Mumbai.
  • India shares its borders with Pakistan to the northwest, China and Nepal to the north, Bhutan to the northeast, and Bangladesh and Myanmar to the east.
  • The Indian Ocean lies to the south of the country, providing India with a long coastline.
  • India is a federal parliamentary democratic republic, with a President as the head of state and a Prime Minister as the head of government.
  • It comprises 28 states and 8 Union territories.
  • The economy of India is one of the fastest-growing major economies in the world, with diverse sectors including agriculture, manufacturing, services, and technology.
  • India is also known for its rich history, ancient civilizations, and contributions to art, science, and philosophy.
  • It is home to several UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including the Taj Mahal, Qutub Minar, and Red Fort.
  • The country celebrates a variety of festivals and events, reflecting its cultural diversity and traditions.
